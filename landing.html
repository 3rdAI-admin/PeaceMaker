The Science

Why it works

10x

fewer flashbacks reported in the Oxford visuospatial intervention study

70%

of participants symptom-free at six months follow-up

NHS

adopted for frontline healthcare workers during the COVID-19 pandemic

Visuospatial tasks performed during the window of memory reconsolidation compete with traumatic imagery for working-memory resources. This interference weakens the vividness and emotional charge of intrusive flashbacks, reducing their frequency over time.

Holmes et al., 2009 — Oxford Study  ·  Iyadurai et al., 2020 — Cambridge ICTI Trial

How It Works

Three gentle steps

1

Focus on the shapes

Engage your visual processing system. Rotating and placing blocks occupies the same mental workspace that intrusive images compete for.

2

Breathe with the guide

A gentle breathing guide helps regulate your nervous system, lowering physiological arousal and creating a sense of safety.

3

Let your mind settle

Extended, unhurried play builds cognitive resilience. There is no rush, no failure state — only presence.

Therapeutic Design

Every detail serves your wellbeing

No game over

Play continues as long as you need. The experience adapts to you.

No scoring pressure

Points and competition are removed. Your only goal is calm.

Shapes slow down

Pieces gradually decelerate over time, easing you into a meditative pace.

Breathing guide

Visual and optional voice coaching to help regulate your breath.

Gentle affirmations

Quiet, supportive messages appear naturally during play.

Ambient soundscape

Layered, calming audio designed to reduce tension and promote focus.

Important Note

A tool, not a treatment

PeaceMaker is a supplementary tool, not a replacement for professional therapy.

For best results, use under clinical guidance or as a complementary coping strategy alongside evidence-based treatment.

If you are experiencing trauma or psychological distress, please reach out to a qualified mental health professional.
